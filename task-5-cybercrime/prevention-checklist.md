# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 – Part B**

## Simple Tips to Stay Safe Online (For College Students)

I made this checklist after learning about how cybercrimes actually happen. These are not complicated rules — they are just small habits that can save you a lot of trouble.

---

### Passwords and Accounts

- [ ] **Use a strong password for every account.** Don't use your name, birthday, or "1234" as a password. Mix letters, numbers and symbols. Yes it's a little annoying but it's worth it.
- [ ] **Turn on two-step verification (2FA) wherever you can.** This means even if someone gets your password, they still can't log in without your phone. Most apps like Gmail and Instagram have this option in settings.
- [ ] **Never share your OTP with anyone.** Not even someone who says they are calling from your bank or from Google. Real companies never ask for OTP over a phone call — ever.

---

### UPI and Money Safety *(Important — please read carefully)*

- [ ] **You don't need to enter your PIN to receive money.** This is the most common mistake people make. Your UPI PIN is only for sending money. If someone is asking you to "enter your PIN to accept a payment," they are scamming you. Just close the app and don't do it.
- [ ] **Always read what the UPI screen is saying before you approve anything.** Look carefully — does it say "Pay" or "Receive"? A collect request will take money out of your account, not put money in. Take 5 seconds to read before pressing confirm.

---

### Links and Fake Messages

- [ ] **Don't click on random links sent on WhatsApp or SMS.** Messages like "You won a prize, click here" or "Your account will be blocked, verify now" are almost always fake. Delete them.
- [ ] **Check the email address properly before trusting any email.** Scammers use fake addresses that look real — like `helpdesk@sbi-support.net` instead of the actual `@sbi.co.in`. If something looks off, it probably is.
- [ ] **Only download apps from Play Store or App Store.** If someone sends you an APK file or a link to download an app, don't install it. These can steal your data or access your banking apps.

---

### Social Media and Personal Information

- [ ] **Don't put your phone number or address on public posts.** If you are selling something on OLX or in a college group, be careful about what personal details you share openly.
- [ ] **Set your social media profiles to private.** You don't need strangers seeing your photos, location or daily routine. Go to your settings and limit who can view your profile.
- [ ] **Be careful with "job offers" in WhatsApp groups.** If someone is offering you money just to like videos or forward messages, it's a scam. Legitimate jobs don't work like that.

---

### Basic Device Safety

- [ ] **Always lock your phone.** Use a PIN or fingerprint. If your phone gets stolen and has no lock, the thief can access your UPI apps, email and everything else within minutes.
- [ ] **Avoid doing bank transactions on public Wi-Fi.** Free Wi-Fi at cafes, railway stations or malls can be unsafe. Use your mobile data for anything related to money or important accounts.

---

## If Something Happens, Report It Here

| Where to Report | How |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | Go to [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) and file a complaint online |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | Call **1930** — available 24 hours, 7 days a week |
| **Your nearest police station** | For serious cases, file an FIR in person |

> **Important:** If money is taken from your account through fraud, call **1930 immediately.** The faster you report, the better the chance they can freeze the transaction before the money disappears.
