# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 – Part A**

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It targets everyday smartphone users — particularly students, young adults, and first-time digital payment users — by exploiting their trust and limited awareness of how UPI actually works.

---

## Realistic Scenario

Arjun, a first-year B.Tech student in Bhopal, listed his old bicycle for sale on OLX. A few hours later, he received a call from someone claiming to be an interested buyer. The caller said he would pay ₹3,500 immediately via Google Pay and asked Arjun to share his UPI ID.

Moments later, Arjun received a notification that said "₹3,500 – Payment Request." Believing it was a payment received, he entered his UPI PIN to "confirm." His bank immediately sent an SMS: ₹3,500 had been debited from his account — not credited.

The fraudster had sent a **collect/payment request**, not a transfer. By entering his PIN, Arjun authorised the outgoing payment himself.

---

## How It Typically Happens — Step by Step

1. Victim posts something for sale online or responds to a job/scholarship post.
2. Fraudster contacts them, posing as a buyer, employer, or government officer.
3. Fraudster says: *"I'm sending you money — just confirm on your app."*
4. Victim receives a UPI **collect request** (which looks like an incoming payment).
5. Victim enters their UPI PIN, believing they are accepting money.
6. Money is instantly debited from the victim's account.
7. Fraudster is unreachable immediately after.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted

- Students selling items on OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or WhatsApp groups
- People responding to part-time job or work-from-home offers
- Individuals receiving fake lottery, scholarship, or cashback notifications
- First-time UPI users unfamiliar with the difference between "send" and "receive"

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often ₹500 – ₹50,000 per incident)
- Emotional distress, embarrassment, and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty in recovering funds — UPI transactions are near-instant and hard to reverse
- In some cases, victims share OTPs or screenshots, leading to further account compromise
