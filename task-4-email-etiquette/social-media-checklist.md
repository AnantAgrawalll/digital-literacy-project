# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
 
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 – Part B**
 
---
 
## ✅ DO's
 
1. **Keep your profiles professional.**
   Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that you would be comfortable showing a future employer or professor.
 
2. **Think before you post.**
   Ask yourself: "Would I be okay if my college, parents, or a recruiter saw this?" If not, don't post it.
 
3. **Use privacy settings actively.**
   Review and update the privacy settings on each platform regularly. Restrict who can see your personal posts, location, and contact information.
 
4. **Give credit when you share others' content.**
   Always tag or mention the original creator when reposting someone else's work, ideas, or images.
 
5. **Fact-check before sharing news or information.**
   Verify information from at least one reliable source before forwarding or reposting. Sharing misinformation — even accidentally — can harm your reputation.
 
6. **Keep your login credentials secure.**
   Use strong, unique passwords for each platform and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever available.
 
7. **Be respectful and constructive in comments and discussions.**
   Healthy debate is welcome; personal attacks, slurs, or harassment are not — regardless of how strongly you feel about a topic.
 
8. **Log out of shared or public devices.**
   Always sign out of your social media accounts when using a lab computer, library terminal, or a friend's device.
 
---
 
## ❌ DON'Ts
 
1. **Don't share personal or sensitive information publicly.**
   Never post your phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar number, bank details, or daily schedule on public profiles or open groups.
 
2. **Don't engage with or spread hate speech, harassment, or bullying.**
   Liking, sharing, or commenting on hateful content makes you part of the problem — even if you did not create it.
 
3. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers without caution.**
   Fake or impersonator accounts are common. If you don't recognise someone, check their profile carefully before accepting.
 
4. **Don't post during moments of anger or strong emotion.**
   Emotional posts are difficult to take back. If something upsets you, wait before responding — or step away entirely.
 
5. **Don't use the same password across multiple platforms.**
   If one account is compromised, a shared password puts all your other accounts at risk.
 
6. **Don't overshare your real-time location.**
   Posting live location updates (e.g., "at the airport right now" or "home alone this weekend") can be a safety risk.
 
7. **Don't participate in or share unverified viral challenges.**
   Some viral trends are dangerous, illegal, or designed to steal data. Verify the source before participating.
 
8. **Don't ignore reports of cyberbullying or online harassment.**
   If you witness someone being harassed online, report the content to the platform and, if serious, inform a trusted adult or use the National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** or **cybercrime.gov.in**.
